In 2016, ReFED’s Roadmap to Reduce U.S. Food Waste by 20 Percent showed the viability of achieving a 20% reduction of food waste within a decade through 27 cost-effective, feasible, and scalable solutions that would divert 13 million tons from landfills and onfarm losses.

Download the Roadmap

Now, our new ReFED Insights Engine offers more than 40 solutions - some breakthrough, some basic, many with the potential for economic benefits – to achieve a 50% reduction in US food waste by 2030, in accordance with goals from the US Government and the United Nations. Combining more than 50 public and proprietary datasets, plus self-reported data from food businesses, our Insights Engine enables users to:

  • Understand the challenge of food waste at an unprecedented level of detail

  • Identify the investments made and needed to reach the highest diversion potential

  • Connect with solutions providers to help scale innovative interventions

  • Calculate the impact of their own waste and potential solutions; and more.

Food waste reduction solutions can be analyzed using a range of factors, including:

  • Annual Diversion Potential – The amount of food waste (tons, pounds, equivalent meals) being reduced
  • Annual Net Economic Value – The aggregate financial benefit to society (consumers, businesses, governments, and other stakeholders) minus all investment and costs.
  • Investment Needed Over Ten Years – The amounf of money needed to implement the solution at scale.
  • Annual GHGs Reduced – The amount in tons of CO2e (carbon dioxide emissions) reduced by each solution.
  • Annual Jobs Created – Food waste reduction solutions are a strong engine for job creation.

There are three categories of food waste reduction solutions:

The solutions were analyzed using the EPA Food Recovery Hierarchy — which prioritizes prevention first, then recovery, and finally recycling — as a starting point. Additional filters of economic value and feasibility were incorporated to understand the potential for scaling solutions.

  • Prevention Solutions

    The first priority for effective management of food waste is prevention. Prevention is applicable across the value chain — from farms to homes.

    Learn More

  • Recovery Solutions

    Food recovery captures that food and transports it while it is still edible to help address the issue of food insecurity

    Learn More

  • Recycling Solutions

    Recycling offers the most scalable path to reducing food waste nationally. Action taken by a handful of large cities alone can prevent millions of tons of food scraps from being landfilled.
