The ReFED Food Waste Policy Finder is an interactive tool for exploring federal and state policies that affect food waste reduction and diversion. Find out which states have effective policy to encourage food donation, learn about states that are leading on recycling policy, and discover how proposed federal policy could clear up consumer confusion around date labeling.

Explore State Policy

Policy treatment of food waste diversion differs dramatically throughout the country. Use this tool to to compare current state policies, identify best practices, and examine recommendations for improvements.

Open State Policy Tool >

Understand Federal Policy

Reaching long-term food waste reduction goals will likely require comprehensive federal food waste policy that sets national guidelines. Use this tool to understand the current federal policy landscape and explore proposals and recommendations for strengthening those policies.

Spotlight on Date Labeling Regulations

A patchwork of state regulations around date labeling causes confusion that results in the significant waste of safe, wholesome food. Learn about the issues, compare state regulations, and find potential solutions to date labeling policy.

About This Tool

ReFED and the Harvard Law School Food Law and Policy Clinic collaborated to develop the Food Waste Policy Finder, with state date labeling information provided by the Natural Resources Defense Council and animal feed policy information provided by the University of Arkansas School of Law.
