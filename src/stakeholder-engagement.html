text_below_logos_html: >-

By signing on to a Voluntary Agreement, signatories commit to the following:

Commit: Publicly commit to and enact the West Coast Regional goal to reduce and prevent wasted food by 50 percent by 2030.

Measure: Annually measure progress in reducing wasted food over time by submitting an annual Food Waste Report in accordance with a measurement framework defined by the signatories.

Act: Implement meaningful actions that reduce wasted food, with an emphasis on prevention. In addition, participate in pre-competitive and collaborative working groups to share lessons learned and best practices, demonstration projects, and develop solutions to collective challenges

Overview

Engaging and Activating Stakeholders Across the Food System

To meet our 2030 goal of reducing food waste by 50 percent, coordination and collaboration is essential. With our holistic view of the food system, ReFED facilitates this by building high-value partnerships and incentivizing new players into the food waste space, through it’s Stakeholder Engagement team.

ReFED’s Stakeholder Engagement team focuses on convening a network of influential thought leaders and decision-makers across private and public entities to enable collective action around food waste solutions and issues that require a systems-based approach. This includes managing ReFED’s internal and external advisory structures, ReFED’s industry and sector partnerships, and hosting the annual Food Waste Summit.

Food waste represents a unique opportunity to generate a positive financial, social, and environmental return on investment. To make that happen, it will require stakeholders to collaborate across the value chain. We invite you to become part of the solution by engaging with ReFED.
