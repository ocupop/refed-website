The COVID-19 pandemic has completely disrupted the food supply chain, resulting in a surge of on-farm food loss, food business closures, and a growing number of food insecure individuals. In response to these new challenges, we have launched the ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund to quickly deliver vital funding to organizations that can rapidly scale food waste reduction and hunger relief efforts.

The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund is a streamlined, one-stop, rapid response vehicle delivering critical support to this important group of high-impact food waste organizations. 100 percent of what is donated will go directly to well-vetted food waste solution providers on the front lines of the COVID-19 crisis.

We anticipate making a minimum of $1 million of grants over the next 30 days to a portfolio of organizations that will be able to – in the next 90 days –drive impact of more than a total of 10 million pounds of food waste prevented or meals rescued. Our aim is to continue raising up to $10 million of donations that will be quickly regranted by ReFED to organizations in a position to make a major impact over the next few months.

How it Works

The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund will distribute capital to both nonprofit and for-profit organizations with scalable solutions ready to make an immediate impact across the U.S. food system over the next 90 days. 

  • Equity-Free Funding: $50,000 on average in non-recoverable grants will be provided to each awardee. 
  • Awardee Selection: Awards will be determined by ReFED and selected from a pipeline of pre-vetted, high-impact organizations, as well as via a national open call for COVID-19 food waste solutions. 
  • Application Process: One simple, straightforward application with submissions evaluated on a rolling basis using a robust set of eligibility and evaluation criteria. When additional information is deemed necessary, ReFED will follow-up with virtual interviews or data requests.
solution priority focus areas
  • Prevention and recovery of fresh and healthy otherwise wasted food, especially at the farm level
    • Increase the preservation and rescue of food that would otherwise go to waste through new sales markets, donation outlets, or processing techniques. 
  • Elimination of logistical constraints along the entire food supply chain
    • Increase the coordination and distribution of otherwise wasted food on a more frequent basis and in ways that account for the requirements of particular food types and form factors – as well as leveraging idle, available capacity such as cold storage.
  • Last-mile delivery — distributing direct to end recipients, especially vulnerable populations facing food insecurity
    • Increase the delivery of nutritionally-rich food to individuals in a convenient and dignified way.
Eligibility Criteria

ReFED is looking for organizations that: 

  • Are applying on behalf of a social enterprise, benefit corporation, for-profit company, public-private partnership, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, or fiscally-sponsored project headquartered and operating in the U.S.
  • Support the mission of the ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund —  helping prevent and/or rescue U.S. food waste at least by 250 tons of food or 417,000 meals in the next 90 days.
  • Have an annual revenue or operating budget of $500,000 or more.
Grant Application Evaluation Criteria
  • Focus Area: The solution aligns with at least one of the primary focus areas of the ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund.
  • Food Waste Impact: The solution can prevent and/or reduce a significant amount of food, especially healthy and nutritious products, from being wasted in the next 90 days (and hopefully beyond to increase resiliency of the food system long-term).
  • Social Impact: The solution meets the growing and dire needs of populations most heavily impacted by COVID-19, including but not limited to certain geographic locations, the elderly, and workers in the agriculture, hospitality, and healthcare industries.
  • Scalability: Extraordinary vision to drive reach and scale in a sustainable way.
  • Team: Well-positioned with relevant experience, expertise, and partners to succeed in meeting desired outcomes during the COVID-19 crisis, with a preference for organizations that prioritize diversity, equity, and inclusion.
