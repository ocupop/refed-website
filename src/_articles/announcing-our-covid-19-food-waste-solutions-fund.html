The COVID-19 pandemic has completely disrupted the food supply chain, resulting in a surge of on-farm food loss, food business closures, and a growing number of food insecure individuals. In response to these new challenges, ReFED is launching our COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund to

 to quickly deliver vital funding to organizations that can rapidly scale food waste reduction and hunger relief efforts.

ReFED’s COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund is a streamlined, one-stop, rapid response vehicle delivering critical support to this important group of high-impact food waste organizations.ReFED will ensure 100 percent of what is donated will go directly to well-vetted food waste solution providers on the front lines of the COVID-19 crisis.

We anticipate making a minimum of $1 million of grants over the next 30 days. Our aim is to continue raising up to $10M of donations that will be quickly regranted by ReFED to organizations in a position to make a major impact over the next few months.

  • Directly prevent or recover food from being wasted at the farm level,
  • Implement creative solutions to the emerging logistics and distribution barriers, and/or
  • Provide food directly to food insecure individuals and other consumers.

  • Relieve emerging distribution and logistical constraints along the food supply chain

    • Offer transportation solutions that connect food that would otherwise be wasted directly to consumers, especially those that are food insecure
    • Solution Examples: Donation transportation; Donation storage & handling services
How it Works:

The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund will distribute capital to both nonprofit and for-profit organizations with scalable solutions ready to make an immediate impact across the U.S. food system over the next 90 days. 

 

  • Equity-Free Funding: $50,000 on average in non-recoverable grants will be provided to each awardee 
  • Awardee Selection: Awards will be determined by ReFED and selected from a pipeline of pre-vetted, high-impact organizations, as well as via a national open call for COVID-19 food waste solutions 
  • Application Process: One simple, straightforward application with submissions evaluated on a rolling basis using a robust set of eligibility and evaluation criteria. When additional information is deemed necessary, ReFED will follow-up with virtual interviews or data requests
Solution Provider Eligibility

ReFED is looking for organizations that: 

  • Are applying on behalf of a social enterprise, benefit corporation, for-profit company, public-private partnership, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, or fiscally-sponsored project headquartered and operating in the U.S.
  • Support the mission of The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund - helping prevent and/or rescue U.S. food waste at least by 250 tons of food or 417,000 meals in the next 90 days.
  • Have an annual revenue or operating budget of $500,000 or more.
Evaluation Criteria

ReFED has a preference for funding solutions that:

  • Align with at least one of the priority areas of the ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund: on-farm food loss; logistics and distribution; last-mile delivery to consumers, especially those that are food insecure
  • Create significant environmental and/or social impact during and beyond the COVID-19 crisis
  • Have the capacity to be scaled and/or replicated in a sustainable way during and beyond the COVID-19 crisis
  • Are built by teams with the relevant experience and domain expertise to execute the COVID-19 Food Waste Initiative.
Timeline
  • April 29th: Launch of First Application Submission Period
  • May 8th: Close of First Application Submission Period 
  • May, 20+: Announce First Round of Grant Recipients on a Rolling Basis 

Note: ReFED will review submissions on a rolling basis and highly encourage eligible solution providers to submit applications as soon as possible. Down the line, the COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund hopes to have subsequent funding rounds available.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

1. What is ReFED? 

ReFED is a nonprofit with a holistic view of the food system, working to advance solutions to reduce food waste. Since its inception in 2015, ReFED has served as a trusted source for data and insights, a connector of stakeholders across the food system, and a catalyst for innovation and economic opportunity..

2. Why is ReFED launching a COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund? 

In response to the impacts of COVID-19 on our food system — especially the increase in wasted food and the growing number of people facing food insecurity — ReFED is leveraging our expertise and extensive network along all stages of the food supply chain.  To help prepare for the coming months, ReFED launched The COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund to quickly raise and deliver funds to organizations that can rapidly scale their impact.

3. Who is eligible to apply?

ReFED is looking for organizations that: 

  • Are applying on behalf of a social enterprise, benefit corporation, for-profit company, public-private partnership, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, or fiscally-sponsored project headquartered and operating in the U.S.
  • Support the mission of ReFED’s COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund - helping prevent and rescue at least 250 tons or 417,000 meals of otherwise wasted food over the next 90 days
  • Have an annual revenue or operating budget of approximately $500,000 or more 

4. How can I prepare for the application process?

Prospective grantees can download a PDF of the full application here.

5. What evaluation criteria will be used to review my application?

  • Focus Area: The solution aligns with at least one of the primary focus areas of the ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund 
  • Food Waste Impact: The solution can prevent and/or reduce a significant amount of food from being wasted in the next 90 days (and hopefully beyond) 
  • Social Impact: The solution meets the growing and dire needs of populations most heavily impacted by COVID-19, including, but not limited to particular cities and states, the elderly, and workers in agriculture, hospitality, and healthcare industries 
  • Scalability: Extraordinary vision to drive reach and scale in a sustainable way 
  • Team: Well-positioned with relevant experience, expertise, and partners to succeed in meeting desired outcomes during the COVID-19 crisis
