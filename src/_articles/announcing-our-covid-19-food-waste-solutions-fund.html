The COVID-19 pandemic has completely disrupted the food supply chain, resulting in a surge of on-farm food loss, food business closures, and a growing number of food insecure individuals. In response to these new challenges, ReFED is launching our COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund to accelerate the impact of solution providers and new initiatives that could:

  • Directly prevent or recover food from being wasted at the farm level,
  • Implement creative solutions to the emerging logistics and distribution barriers, and/or
  • Provide food directly to food insecure individuals and other consumers.

We’re Most Excited to See Solutions That:

  • Prevent food from being wasted at the farm-level
    • Preserve the value of food in the form of energy and resources
    • Solution Examples: Value-added processing; Secondary resale markets
  • Relieve emerging distribution and logistical constraints along the food supply chain
    • Assist in the coordination and transportation of food that would otherwise go to waste
    • Solution Examples: Donation matching; Transportation coordination 
  • Provide last-mile delivery 
    • Offer transportation solutions that connect food that would otherwise be wasted directly to consumers, especially those that are food insecure
    • Solution Examples: Donation transportation; Donation storage & handling services

How it Works:

The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund is excited to distribute capital to organizations with innovative solutions that can make an immediate impact across the U.S. food system. Grant recipients will receive:

  • Equity-Free Funding: Each participating organization will receive a grant that averages $50,000 in size to scale a solution that prevents and/or rescues food that would otherwise go to waste. 

Solution Provider Eligibility:

ReFED is looking for organizations that: 

  • Are applying on behalf of a social enterprise, benefit corporation, for-profit company, public-private partnership, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, or fiscally-sponsored project headquartered and operating in the U.S.
  • Support the mission of The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund - helping prevent and/or rescue U.S. food waste at least by 250 tons of food or 417,000 meals in the next 90 days
  • Have an annual revenue or operating budget of $500,000 or more.

Evaluation Criteria:

ReFED has a preference for funding solutions that:

  • Align with at least one of the priority areas of the ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund: on-farm food loss; logistics and distribution; last-mile delivery to consumers, especially those that are food insecure
  • Create significant environmental and/or social impact during and beyond the COVID-19 crisis
  • Have the capacity to be scaled and/or replicated in a sustainable way during and beyond the COVID-19 crisis
  • Are built by teams with the relevant experience and domain expertise to execute the COVID-19 Food Waste Initiative.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Background:

What is ReFED? 

  • ReFED is a nonprofit with a holistic view of the food system, working to advance solutions to reduce food waste. Since its inception in 2015, ReFED has served as a source for data and insights, a connector of stakeholders across the food system, and a catalyst for innovation and economic opportunity.

Why is ReFED launching a COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund? 

  • In response to the impacts of COVID-19 on our food system – especially the increase in wasted food that could have gone to the growing number of people facing food insecurity – ReFED is working to leverage our expertise and extensive network at all stages of the food supply chain. The COVID-19 Food Waste Solutions Fund is designed to accelerate the impact of solution providers and new initiatives that could directly prevent or recover food from being wasted at the farm-level; implement creative solutions to the emerging logistics and distribution barriers; and/or provide food directly to food-insecure individuals and other consumers.

Application Process:

Who is eligible to apply?

ReFED is looking for organizations that: 

  • Are applying on behalf of a social enterprise, benefit corporation, for-profit company, public-private partnership, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization, or fiscally-sponsored project headquartered and operating in the U.S.
  • Support the mission of The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund - helping prevent and/or rescue U.S. food waste at least by 250 tons of food or 417,000 meals in the next 90 days.
  • Have an annual revenue or operating budget of $500,000 or more.

What can the grant award be used for?

  • The ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund will offer unrestricted grants that average $50,000 in size, which may be used to help prototype, pilot, or scale a product or service that prevents and/or rescues food that would otherwise be wasted. 

What evaluation criteria will be used to review my application?

  • Focus: The solution aligns with at least one of the priority areas of the ReFED COVID-19 Food Waste Solution Fund: on-farm food loss; logistics and distribution; last-mile delivery to consumers, especially those that are food insecure.
  • Impact: The solution creates significant environmental and/or social impact during and beyond the COVID-19 crisis. The applicant also has a firm grasp on how the impacts of their initiative will be measured. 
  • Scalability: Extraordinary vision to scale the solution in a sustainable way during and beyond the COVID-19 crisis. The applicant articulates a strategy based on evidence to ensure long-term change. 
  • Team: Well-positioned with relevant experience, expertise, and partners to substantially prevent and/or rescue U.S. food waste during and after the COVID-19 crisis.

How will winners be selected?

  • There will be one application round where solution providers will be filtered based on eligibility and their responses to short answer questions. Please note that ReFED reserves the right to schedule a follow-up virtual interview if more information is needed.
