<br>&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &lt;h1 style="display: block;margin: 0;padding: 0;color: #202020;font-family: Helvetica;font-size: 26px;font-style: normal;font-weight: bold;line-height: 125%;letter-spacing: normal;text-align: left;"&gt; &lt;/h1&gt;

&lt;p style="margin: 10px 0;padding: 0;mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #202020;font-family: Helvetica;font-size: 16px;line-height: 150%;text-align: left;"&gt;Dear ReFED Community,&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>It is a pleasure to write to all of you for the first time! As ReFED’s inaugural Executive Director, I’m excited to be joining a dynamic, driven team supported by such an engaged network of experts, practitioners, and visionaries leading the charge to reduce wasted food in the United States. I also want to take this opportunity to publicly thank Sarah Vared for her incredible leadership of ReFED over the past two years as Interim Director.&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;br&gt;
<br>I look forward to working with you in this new chapter of ReFED as we build upon our &lt;em&gt;Roadmap&lt;/em&gt; and, together, take action to reduce wasted food at scale.&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>Last month, ReFED launched groundbreaking tools around two of the four Roadmap levers of action: innovation and policy. The &lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/tools/innovator-database/" target="_blank" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;Food Waste Innovator Database&lt;/a&gt; highlights over 400 innovators who are building new industries to reduce wasted food. The &lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/tools/food-waste-policy-finder/" target="_blank" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;Food Waste Policy Finder&lt;/a&gt; identifies leading and lagging state policies that enable or block food waste reduction. Together, these tools create a one-stop shop to learn about food waste innovation and policy. Learn more about these tools and key insights by watching our latest &lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/content-hub/refed-innovator-database-and-food-policy-tool-webinars" target="_blank" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;webinar&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>In June, we will release a tool for standardized date labeling that will enable food companies to implement the FMI/GMA commitment to standardize date labels. Date labeling is one of the most cost-effective solutions to reduce waste, with the potential to save consumers $29 billion per year by reducing consumer confusion over the meaning of labels. The Date Labeling Tool was developed through a multi-stakeholder process and will be open to public comment. We plan to test and refine the guide with key partners over the summer and finalize the guide this fall for widespread use.&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>Over the next few months, we will be working with retailers, restaurants and food service providers to develop and apply stakeholder-specific action guides. These guides break down the &lt;em&gt;Roadmap&lt;/em&gt; into stakeholder-specific actions, providing new solutions and case studies. We will develop these stakeholder guides with leading companies and help them further implement the key solutions in their businesses. We will release the guides this fall.&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>As we head into the summer, look for updates on upcoming events, including the NYC Food Waste Fair, which ReFED has supported as a host committee member, and the Global Sustainability Summit, where ReFED will convene a solutions workshop for retailers and manufacturers. Later this year, we also plan to release new resources that support our mission to catalyze leading U.S. organizations to harness economic, social, and environmental value from wasted food. &lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>Sincerely,&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>Chris Cochran&lt;br&gt;
<br>Executive Director, ReFED&lt;br&gt;

<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;strong&gt;Food Waste Innovator Database and Policy Finder Launch! &lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/content-hub/press-release" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;On April 18&lt;/a&gt;, ReFED released two new interactive online tools:&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/tools/innovator-database/" target="_blank" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Innovator Database&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;br&gt;
<br>Over 400 commercial and nonprofit entities are turning the problem of food waste into an opportunity for economic, social, and environmental impacts. Explore the dynamic, expanding food waste innovation industry, categorized by solution type and geographic location.&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/tools/food-waste-policy-finder/" target="_blank" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Food Waste Policy Finder&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/a&gt;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;br&gt;
<br>Developed in partnership with Harvard Law School’s Food Law and Policy Clinic, the Policy Finder provides an overview of federal and state food waste laws, including date labeling regulations, liability protections, tax incentives, animal feed regulations, and organic waste bans. The Policy Finder will support advocates and empower policymakers to develop more effective policies to promote food waste prevention, recovery, and recycling.&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>The new tools gained immediate traction on social media and were featured by &lt;a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2017/04/19/the-hot-new-trend-in-food-is-literal-garbage/?utm_term=.772803ce19c3" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;&lt;em&gt;The Washington Post&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/a&gt; and &lt;a href="http://news.nationalpost.com/life/food-drink/stale-bread-beer-and-crooked-carrots-how-the-hottest-trend-in-food-start-ups-is-turning-trash-into-products" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;&lt;em&gt;National Post&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/a&gt;, as well as in blogs from &lt;a href="http://gracelinks.org/blog/7936/refed-creates-two-new-tools-to-fight-food-waste-in-the-us" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;GRACE Communications Foundation&lt;/a&gt;, &lt;a href="http://ht.ly/8Og630aYWZh" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;One Green Planet&lt;/a&gt;, and &lt;a href="http://www.foodfuture.co/blog-harvest/2017/4/24/how-to-unleash-the-food-waste-economy" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;Food Future Co&lt;/a&gt;. To support the launch of the tools, ReFED interviewed Caue Suplicy, Founder and CEO of Barnana, to learn what it’s like to be a pioneer in the ‘Food Product Creation’ space. &lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/content-hub/going-barnanas-for-new-ways-to-use-food" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;Read this&lt;/a&gt; and other ReFED articles in our&lt;em&gt; &lt;/em&gt;new &lt;a href="http://www.refed.com/content-hub#active_tab=news" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;Content Hub&lt;/a&gt;!&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&lt;strong&gt;Opportunities to Reduce Food Waste in the 2018 Farm Bill&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;br&gt;
<br>&nbsp;&lt;br&gt;
<br>ReFED and Food Policy Action recently supported Harvard Law School Food Law and Policy Clinic’s efforts to publish a &lt;a href="http://www.chlpi.org/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/Opportunities-to-Reduce-Food-Waste-in-the-2018-Farm-Bill_May-2017.pdf" target="_blank" style="mso-line-height-rule: exactly;-ms-text-size-adjust: 100%;-webkit-text-size-adjust: 100%;color: #2BAADF;font-weight: normal;text-decoration: underline;"&gt;report&lt;/a&gt; providing recommendations on how the farm bill could be used to address food waste. Passed every five years, the farm bill allocates roughly $500 billion in spending on nutrition and agriculture programs in the U.S., providing a compelling opportunity to promote food waste solutions.&lt;br&gt;
