A cost-effective approach to food waste

ReFED set out to understand the most cost-effective ways to reduce food waste and the resources needed to implement those solutions at scale.

Read more about ReFED's methodology  >
ReFED developed a core economic model to perform three analyses on the solutions:
  1. A total net societal “Economic Value” based on their cost-effectiveness per ton of waste
  2. The business profit potential that the private sector can earn by investing in each solution
  3. The non-financial impacts of each solution — meals saved, greenhouse gases reduced, jobs created, water conserved

Profiting From Food Waste Solutions

Eleven of the solutions represent $1.9 billion in business profit potential. Nine are geared toward prevention and two toward recycling. They all create revenue and cost savings opportunities that can be captured by the businesses that implement them.

Restaurants and foodservice facilities have the largest profit opportunity.
