A range of solutions to reduce food waste already exist – some are breakthrough, some are basic, and importantly, many have a strong potential for investment returns. ReFED estimates that an investment of $YY over the next ten years will reduce food waste by XX tons, which will then avoid ZZ tons of GHGs, provide the ability to rescue AA meals, create BB jobs, and deliver CC in economic value to the country. And many businesses, cities, nonprofits, and others are already making a substantial impact to address the problem.

But much more needs to be done.

The Path Forward

Roadmap to 2030: Reducing US Food Waste by 50%

ReFED’s Roadmap 2030 and the ReFED Insights Engine was specifically developed to provide food system players with everything they need to move from awareness about the problem to action.

Building on our landmark 2016 Roadmap to Reduce US Food Waste by 20%, this new action plan provides details on more than 40 solutions to reduce food waste.

The end result?

We’re committed to generating the awareness and action needed to achieve a 50% reduction in food waste by the year 2030 in accordance with national and international goals – and to creating a sustainable, inclusive, and resilient food system that optimizes environmental resources, minimizes climate impacts, and makes the best use of the food we grow.
