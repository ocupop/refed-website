On October 18, 2018, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (“EPA”), the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (“FDA”), and U.S. Department of Agriculture (“USDA”) signed a formal agreement with ReFED committing to increase collaboration and coordination in areas of mutual interest relating to the reduction of food loss and waste. 

text_below_logos_html: >-

The formal agreement indicates that:

  1. The Federal Parties intend to identify and develop non-exclusive opportunities for collaboration between ReFED and federal, state, territorial, tribal, and local government officials in efforts to reduce food waste in the United States.
  2. The Federal Parties intend to consult as appropriate with ReFED to develop approaches for measuring the success of various strategies and techniques currently being deployed nationwide to reduce food waste. 
  3. The Federal Parties intend to participate as appropriate in the Further with Food: Center for Food Loss and Waste Solutions (https://furtherwithfood.org) public-private partnership, where ReFED serves as the Host. 
  4. The Parties intend to leverage existing platforms and partnerships to advance data collection and measurement activities related to food loss and waste. 
  5. The Parties expect to meet periodically to provide updates on progress being made in food waste reduction efforts and meet at minimum bi-yearly throughout the life of this formal agreement. 
  6. ReFED intends to annually provide a two-page summary of observations from that calendar year of strides being made to reduce food waste across the country. The summary is anticipated to also identify where action or involvement by the Federal Parties may result in reduction of food loss and waste. 
  7. The Parties intend to share this formal agreement, including a link to this agreement, on relevant websites and/or social media. 
  8. Subsequent to the signing of this formal agreement, the Parties expect to identify a more detailed list of actions and responsibilities that reflect the above terms.

Overview

Mobilizing and connecting people to take targeted action

Food waste represents a unique opportunity to generate a positive financial, social, and environmental return on investment. To make that happen, it will require stakeholders to collaborate and coordinate across the value chain. ReFED facilitates this by building high-value partnerships and incentivizing new players into the food waste space, through it’s Stakeholder Engagement team.

ReFED’s Stakeholder Engagement team focuses on convening a network of influential thought leaders and decision-makers across private and public entities to enable collective action around food waste solutions and issues that require a systems-based approach. This includes managing ReFED’s internal and external advisory structures, ReFED’s industry and sector partnerships, and hosting the annual Food Waste Summit.

Networks

ReFED’s strength has long rested in the power of the people who surround, advise and contribute to our work. Our Advisory Council and Expert Network are comprised of influential leaders across the food system,  who inform the organization’s strategic initiatives, offer technical expertise, and collectively provide a holistic analysis of the challenges and opportunities for reducing wasted food.
