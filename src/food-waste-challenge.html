Each year, 52.4 million tons of food are thrown into landfills annually, and another 10.1 million tons is estimated to be discarded or left unharvested on farms and in packinghouses.

This mountain of wasted calories, resources, and energy grows up to two times larger when you add in other food fit for human consumption yet still ends up being composted, converted into animal feed, or discarded in other ways.

The good news?

Food waste is a solvable problem — And we know how to do it.

Where does food waste occur?

Addressable food waste can be found throughout the supply chain, which can be simplified into four segments: farms, food manufacturers, consumer-facing businesses (including distributors, retail grocers, restaurants, foodservice providers, and institutions), and homes (including all types of dwellings).
