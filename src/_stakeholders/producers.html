Food loss starts at the production level. Low market prices and high labor costs often make it uneconomical for farmers to harvest all that they produce. Strict cosmetic standards result in insufficient demand for imperfect-looking produce. Despite gleaning and farm-to-food-bank efforts to recover this unharvested food, the vast majority is left in the fields and tilled under. Additionally, supply chain disruptions caused by COVID-19 closures and other barriers, along with labor shortages caused by virus precautions, led to an uptick of waste at the farm level early on during the pandemic.

Food waste reduction solutions for Producers cover agricultural production and are inclusive of enterprises that package agricultural or animal products at the site of production. Farming generates 23.8M tons of surplus food, nearly all of which is produce that is left behind after harvesting because it’s not considered marketable. Only 1.2% is donated for hunger relief.

As we are increasingly removed from our agricultural system, it’s important to reestablish connections from consumers all the way back to the farm where food originates. Implementing solutions and establishing best practices at the farm level can build resiliency throughout the entire supply chain.
