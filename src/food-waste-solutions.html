A range of solutions to reduce food waste already exist – some are breakthrough, some are basic, and importantly, many have a strong potential for investment returns. ReFED estimates that an investment of $YY over the next ten years will reduce food waste by XX tons, which will then avoid ZZ tons of GHGs, provide the ability to rescue AA meals, create BB jobs, and deliver CC in economic value to the country. And many businesses, cities, nonprofits, and others are already making a substantial impact to address the problem.

But much more needs to be done.

Food Waste Action Guide

Our new Roadmap 2030 outlines seven priority areas across the food system where we must focus our efforts over the next ten years. Building on our landmark 2016 Roadmap to Reduce US Food Waste by 20%, this new action plan provides details on more than 40 solutions to reduce food waste. What’s more, Roadmap 2030 is powered by the brand new ReFED Insights Engine, an online hub for data and insights; analysis of available solutions; recommended best practices; a directory of potential partners; and more. In line with the United Nations General Assembly’s recommended three-step approach of target, measure, and act to achieve a 50% reduction in food waste by 2030, ReFED’s Roadmap 2030 and the ReFED Insights Engine were specifically developed to provide food system players with everything they need to move from awareness about the problem to action.
